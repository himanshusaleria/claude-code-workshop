# Product Discovery Call — Transcript

**Date**: March 25, 2026
**Customer**: Ravi Mehta, Head of Design at BuildCraft Interiors
**Our Side**: Neha (PM), Arjun (Solutions Engineer)
**Duration**: 22 minutes
**Tool**: Fathom / Zoom

---

**Neha**: Thanks for taking the time, Ravi. We'd love to understand how your team currently handles interior design projects and where you're running into friction.

**Ravi**: Sure. So we're a team of 12 designers working on commercial and residential projects. Right now our biggest pain point is collaboration. We use a mix of AutoCAD, SketchUp, and some manual drawings. Every designer has their own preferred tool, so when we need to share work or hand off a project, it's a mess.

**Neha**: When you say hand off, what does that look like today?

**Ravi**: Someone exports their drawings as PDFs or DWG files, uploads to Google Drive, then the next person imports into their tool. We lose data every time. Dimensions get messed up, materials don't carry over. Last month we had a project where the kitchen cabinet dimensions were wrong because of a bad export. The client was furious.

**Arjun**: Are you doing any 3D visualization currently?

**Ravi**: Some of us use SketchUp for 3D renders, but it's separate from the floor plan workflow. So we design in 2D, then recreate in 3D — it's double the work. We've been looking at tools that do both, which is how we found you guys.

**Neha**: Makes sense. What about the client presentation side? How do you share designs with clients?

**Ravi**: We export renders as images, put them in a PowerPoint, and present on a call. Clients can't interact with the design. They say "move that sofa to the left" and we go back, make the change, re-render, and schedule another call. For a typical project, we go through 4-5 revision cycles.

**Neha**: Wow, that's a lot of back and forth. If you could wave a magic wand, what would you change?

**Ravi**: Three things. First, one tool that does 2D floor plans AND 3D visualization — no more double work. Second, real-time collaboration so two designers can work on the same project. Third, some way for clients to see designs in 3D and leave comments directly, like Figma does for UI design.

**Arjun**: We can actually do all three of those. Let me show you a quick demo of the collaboration feature...

**Ravi**: Oh wait, one more thing. Pricing. We need to generate BOQs — bill of quantities — from the design. Right now our project manager manually counts every item from the drawing and creates a spreadsheet. Takes 2-3 days per project. If the design changes, the BOQ is wrong again.

**Neha**: That's a great point. Our platform auto-generates BOQs from the design — when you place a cabinet or a light fixture, it's automatically added to the quantity sheet with pricing.

**Ravi**: That alone would save us probably 10 hours per project. How many projects can we have running simultaneously?

**Neha**: Unlimited. And each project has version history, so you can always go back to a previous design.

**Ravi**: What about our existing AutoCAD files? We have 3 years of project files.

**Arjun**: We support DWG import. There's some cleanup needed depending on how the files are structured, but most standard AutoCAD files import cleanly.

**Ravi**: Okay. Pricing — what does this look like for a team of 12?

**Neha**: I'll send over a detailed proposal, but our team plan starts at ₹2,500 per user per month. For 12 users we can discuss volume pricing.

**Ravi**: That's within budget. Can we do a pilot with 3 designers first? I want to test the AutoCAD import with our real files before committing the whole team.

**Neha**: Absolutely. We'll set up a pilot with dedicated onboarding support. I'll send you the details by end of day tomorrow.

**Ravi**: Perfect. Thanks, Neha and Arjun. Looking forward to it.
